# Wiki Philosophy

Wiki is not "nice-to-have docs".

It is a compounding knowledge layer between raw canonical records and repeated questions.

## Why this matters

Without a wiki, the agent keeps rediscovering the same synthesis:

- "how does Ralph really work here?"
- "what does accepted packet discipline look like?"
- "why do tickets outrank plans?"
- "how should critique feed back into execution?"

With a wiki, those answers become durable pages that can be maintained over time.

## Source rule

A wiki page should be grounded in accepted records and evidence.

If the answer is still speculative, keep it in tickets, research, or critique until it stabilizes.

## Page quality

A good wiki page is:

- clear
- well-scoped
- interlinked
- sourced
- updated when reality changes
